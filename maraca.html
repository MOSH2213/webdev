Maraca



A maraca (pronunciation (help·info)), sometimes called rumba shaker or chac-chac,[1] is a rattle which appears in many genres of Caribbean and Latin music.

It is shaken by a handle and usually played as part of a pair. Maracas (from Guaraní mbaracás),[2] also known as tamaracas, were rattles of divination, an oracle of the Brazilian Tupinamba indigenous, found also with other native tribes, such as the Guarani, Orinoco and in Florida. A maraca player in Spanish is a maraquero.
